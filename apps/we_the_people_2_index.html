Civic action toolkit

How to research a law

  1. Identify topic and jurisdiction.
  2. Start with primary law: Constitution, statute, regulation, case.
  3. Use official sources first.
  4. Check dates, amendments, repeals, and current status.
  5. Separate fact, law, opinion, and advocacy.

How to comment on a rule

  1. Find the docket on Regulations.gov.
  2. Read the proposed rule and agency questions.
  3. Explain who you are and how the rule affects you.
  4. Use facts, examples, and legal/statutory references.
  5. Submit before the deadline and save confirmation.

How to evaluate a claim

  1. Ask: text, statute, regulation, case, or commentary?
  2. Find the primary source.
  3. Check whether it is current.
  4. Look for competing interpretations.
  5. State uncertainty clearly.

Student research template

Facts: What happened? Issue: What constitutional question arises? Hook: Which Article or Amendment? Law: What statute/regulation/case matters? Source: Which official source confirms it? Conclusion: What can be said confidently, and what needs more research?
